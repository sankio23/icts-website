§ Research Units

Six environments exploring questions that move between disciplines.

Each unit addresses a distinct domain of inquiry while remaining open to the others. Researchers regularly hold appointments across more than one unit, and projects are designed to circulate between them.

  • LOGOS

    History of Christianity and Christian Thought

    LOGOS investigates the historical development of Christianity and the evolution of Christian thought across cultural, philosophical, and institutional contexts. The unit focuses on how theological and intellectual traditions have shaped social, political, and cultural structures over time, and how they continue to influence contemporary forms of knowledge and collective life.

    Enter unit →

  • RAISE

    Responsible Artificial Intelligence and Social Ethics

    RAISE addresses the problem of responsibility in AI-driven societies. It investigates how responsibility can be articulated within socio-technical systems where agency is distributed across human and artificial actors, moving beyond purely regulatory or principle-based approaches.

    Enter unit →

  • CI²

    Complexity & Information Initiative

    CI² investigates how information, cognition, and agency emerge in complex systems. It integrates philosophy of science, physics, and cognitive sciences to develop a unified framework for understanding knowledge as a relational and dynamic phenomenon, with particular attention to quantum computers and the foundations of quantum computation.

    Enter unit →

  • PRISM

    Peace, Risk, and International Security & Mediation

    PRISM analyzes conflict, security, and diplomacy in a global context, focusing on mediation, trust-building, and relational approaches to international interaction beyond purely strategic models.

    Enter unit →

  • FRATERNITAS

    Fraternity, Politics, and Social Theory

    FRATERNITAS develops fraternity as a foundational category for political and social thought, investigating how relational concepts can reshape institutions, social bonds, and democratic life in globally interconnected societies.

    Enter unit →

  • OIKOS

    Family, Generations & Communion — CISVF

    OIKOS investigates family life as a complex, intergenerational, and intercultural reality. Bringing together representatives of international family and youth networks, the unit studies the family from multiple, deeply interconnected perspectives — those of adults and those of younger generations.
