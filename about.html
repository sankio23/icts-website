§ Orientation

A grammar for inquiry across boundaries.

The International Center for Transdisciplinary Studies (ICTS) is an inter-university research center that promotes a relational transdisciplinary approach to the study of complex contemporary challenges. By bringing together scholars, institutions, and diverse fields of knowledge, ICTS advances research, education, and international scientific cooperation.

Mission

To form a community capable of holding rigour and openness in the same gesture — disciplinary depth on one hand, the courage to listen across traditions on the other.

We pursue collaborative research, host seminars, publish original work, and form younger scholars in habits of attention that are simultaneously technical and humane. The Center is small by intention. Its weight is meant to be intellectual, not administrative.

Vision

A research culture in which the boundaries between disciplines are treated not as walls but as thresholds — places where meaning is renewed, not lost.

On Transdisciplinarity

Multidisciplinarity sets disciplines side by side. Interdisciplinarity borrows methods between them. Transdisciplinarity goes further: it asks what lies between, across, and beyond — and what the human being looks like when seen from that vantage.

We work from the conviction that reality is layered, that knowledge is one of those layers, and that no single discipline holds a complete map of the territory. To think transdisciplinarily is to refuse the false choice between fragmentation and uniformity. It is to seek complex unity — a unity that does not erase difference but orders it.

This commitment shapes how we run seminars, how we choose collaborators, and how we measure our work. It is closer to a habit of mind than a method.

Institutional Ground

ICTS is a research center of Sophia University Institute, a pontifical university in Loppiano, Italy. Sophia was founded as a place where academic disciplines and lived experience would inform one another — and where dialogue between cultures and faiths would not be an extracurricular activity but a constitutive method.

The Center extends this orientation outward, working with universities, research institutes, ecclesial bodies, and civil society partners across Europe, the Americas, Africa, and Asia.

"The work of thought is to inhabit the seam — to stay there long enough that what each side cannot say alone begins, slowly, to be sayable."
ICTS

Knowledge does not belong to disciplines. It emerges in the relations that traverse them.
